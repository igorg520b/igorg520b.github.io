Background

I am a researcher working on numerical models for deformation, crushing and flow of ice. The models are based on continuum mechanics, where numerical approaches include particle-based methods and finite elements.

Projects in which I am involved use unique model formulations and custom implementations. I got introduced to GPGPU/CUDA when performing Lattice Boltzmann simulations for my Master's Thesis. Parallel computation carried on to my Ph.D. work, where FEM simulations were conducted for modeling of ice sample fracture. Over time I established a set of favorite programming tools: Qt, VTK, MKL, MOSEK, HDF5, Boost and a few others.

My language of choice used to be C#, as it steers programmers away from making common errors. After getting more involved with scientific computation, where the use of 3rd party libraries is often necessary, I switched to the plain (new) C++, which is a common denominator for many APIs.

Occasionally I had to delve into Fortran code, which still has its place in scientific computation and makes part of some FEM tools. I even taught a full course titled Introduction to Scientific Computing, which focused on Fortran.

My most recent involvement is the SIIBED project with C-CORE that investigates subsea interaction between pipelines and icebergs.

Projects / Publications

Numerical Simulation for Subsea Ice Interaction Barriers to Energy Development (SIIBED)

The partner organization, C-CORE, conducts medium- and large-scale experiments on ice fracture. The goal is to estimate the mechanical loads involved in the interaction of icebergs and subsea cables/pipelines.
